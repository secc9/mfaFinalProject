      - Fell makes four points regarding the making of tools
	1) toolmaking is a good test of skill
	2) having high quality tools is important because their quality impacts everything that is made with them
	3) toolmaking offers an increased understanding of the function of a particular tool, its means of construction, and the materials and processes that went into its making
	4) the toolmaker gains a unique awareness of the tool's idiosyncracies, which enhances their use of it
    - Regarding
      1) tool making IS a good test of skill. it combines the makers efforts into building things that allow them to make works, gradually epxanding skills and works g hand in hand. I think a problem is also how to depart from what came before.
      2) High quality tools are important. you need robustness. YOu need to know that your machine is going to work time after time, but as an aside to this, poorer quality also can intruduce unexpected results, not only aesthetically, but also conceptually. Having to repeatedly rebuild and make tools pushes skills and knowledge further. The over-relience on the same tool can breed comfort and consistency.
